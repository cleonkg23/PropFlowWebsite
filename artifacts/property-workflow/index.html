The problem

You’re running the business out of inboxes, spreadsheets and memory.

Most small property teams aren’t short on effort. They’re short on visibility. The same patterns show up again and again.

Enquiries handled inconsistently

New leads land in shared inboxes, WhatsApp threads and a personal phone. Some get a reply in an hour. Some get one in three days.

Maintenance buried in email

A tenant reports a leak. It’s acknowledged, then drifts down the inbox. Nobody’s sure who owns it until the tenant chases again.

Follow-ups quietly missed

Viewings happen, then nothing. Landlord requests get a half answer and no diary entry. Pipeline value leaks out the side of the business.

One person remembers everything

If they’re off, things stop. There’s no shared view of what’s open, what’s waiting, and what’s overdue across the team.

What I automate

Practical workflows, built around how property teams actually work.

I don’t replace your tools. I connect them, and remove the manual steps in between.

New enquiry triage

Capture enquiries from email, web forms and portals into one place. Categorise by intent — tenant, landlord, viewing, maintenance — and route to the right person.

Viewing follow-up workflows

Automatically create follow-up tasks after every viewing, with reminders if no response within your chosen window.

Maintenance request logging

Turn a tenant email or message into a tracked job with owner, status and SLA. Keep tenants updated without anyone retyping the same reply.

Landlord admin requests

Drafted email responses

Worked example

A real workflow system I built — and what it means for property businesses.

This isn’t a polished case study with invented numbers. It’s an honest description of a prototype I built to replace messy manual tracking, and how the same approach translates to property.

The problem

Work was being tracked across email, chat and a couple of spreadsheets. Items got picked up twice, dropped, or quietly forgotten. Nobody could answer “what’s actually open right now?” without asking three people.

The approach

Map the real workflow as it’s used — not as it’s described. Identify where information is being copied by hand. Replace those handoffs with a single source of truth and lightweight automation around it.

What the system did

Captured incoming work into one queue. Assigned ownership automatically. Generated reminders for ageing items. Surfaced status on a single shared view, so anyone could see what was open, blocked, or overdue.

Why this matters for property

The same pattern fits estate agencies and property management almost directly: enquiries, viewings, maintenance jobs, landlord requests, document chasing. Centralised visibility, clearer next actions, fewer dropped balls, more consistent follow-up.

Treat this as proof of practical capability, not a packaged product. Each engagement is built around your actual workflow.
