§ 01 — The problem

You’re running the business out of inboxes, spreadsheets and memory.

Most small property teams aren’t short on effort. They’re short on visibility. The same patterns turn up again and again.

i.

Enquiries handled inconsistently

New leads land in shared inboxes, on WhatsApp, and on a personal phone. Some get answered in an hour. Some take three days.

ii.

Maintenance buried in email

A tenant reports a leak. It’s acknowledged, then drifts down the inbox. Nobody’s sure who owns it until they chase again.

iii.

Follow-ups quietly missed

Viewings happen, then nothing. Landlord requests get a half answer and no diary entry. Pipeline value leaks out the side of the business.

iv.

One person remembers everything

If they’re off, things stop. There’s no shared view of what’s open, what’s waiting, and what’s overdue across the team.

§ 02 — What I automate

Practical workflows, built around how property teams actually work.

I don’t replace your tools. I connect them — and remove the manual steps in between.

New enquiry triage

Capture enquiries from email, web forms and portals into one place. Categorise by intent — tenant, landlord, viewing, maintenance — and route to the right person.

Viewing follow-ups

Automatically create follow-up tasks after every viewing, with reminders if there’s no response within your chosen window.

Maintenance request logging

Turn a tenant email or message into a tracked job with owner, status and SLA. Keep tenants updated without anyone retyping the same reply.

Landlord admin requests

Capture statement requests, certificate questions and instructions in one queue, with clear ownership and a paper trail.

Chasing missing information

References, ID, deposit details, signed agreements — chased automatically on a schedule until they’re returned.

Internal handoffs

When a deal moves stage, the next person gets a clean task with everything they need — instead of a forwarded email thread.

Status & pipeline tracking

One shared view of every enquiry, viewing, application and maintenance job — updated automatically as work happens.

Drafted email responses

Suggested replies for common situations — viewing confirmations, maintenance acknowledgements, document chasers — ready to review and send.

Lightweight reporting

Optional dashboards on top of the workflows: response times, open jobs by branch, pipeline value, what’s overdue.

§ 04 — Worked example

A real workflow system I built — and what it means for property businesses.

This isn’t a polished case study with invented numbers. It’s an honest description of a prototype I built to replace messy manual tracking, and how the same approach translates to property.

Same operating principles. Different front door.
The problem

Work scattered across email, chat and spreadsheets.

Items got picked up twice, dropped, or quietly forgotten. Nobody could answer “what’s actually open right now?” without asking three people.

The approach

Map the workflow as it’s used — not as it’s described.

Find where information is being copied by hand. Replace those handoffs with a single source of truth and lightweight automation around it.

What the system did

One queue, automatic ownership, ageing reminders, shared visibility.

Captured incoming work into one queue. Assigned ownership automatically. Generated reminders for ageing items. Surfaced status on a shared view, so anyone could see what was open, blocked, or overdue.

Why this matters for property

The same pattern fits estate agencies and property management almost directly.

Enquiries, viewings, maintenance jobs, landlord requests, document chasing — centralised visibility, clearer next actions, fewer dropped balls, more consistent follow-up.

Treat this as proof of practical capability, not a packaged product. Each engagement is built around your actual workflow.
