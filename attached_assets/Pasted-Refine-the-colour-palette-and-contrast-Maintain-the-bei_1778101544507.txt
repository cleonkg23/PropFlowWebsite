Refine the colour palette and contrast. Maintain the beige‑and‑green scheme—earthy palettes like forest green and sandy beige are on trend and communicate calm and authenticity. However, limit the palette to two or three colours and introduce a darker accent shade (such as a saturated green or blue) to highlight important elements. Replace light grey text with darker tones so that captions, body copy and buttons stand out more clearly; forum users complain that fading grey text on beige is hard to focus on.
Shorten and re‑frame the hero message. Replace the current long headline with a concise, outcome‑focused statement (e.g., “Never miss a tenant update again”). Studies show visitors decide whether to stay within seconds and that minimalist designs must communicate brand messages quickly. Pair the headline with a subheading that succinctly explains what PropFlow does and who it serves.
Add a story‑driven visual in the hero section. Modern SaaS pages use story‑driven hero sections with visual cues to illustrate the problem‑solution narrative. Rather than a static task board, embed a short animation or GIF showing a tenant email becoming a task and automatically notifying a landlord. This helps visitors understand the workflow immediately.
Introduce micro‑animations and subtle interactions. Purposeful micro‑animations, such as hover effects on buttons, smooth scroll‑reveals for sections or animated checkmarks when form fields are validated, enhance engagement without distracting users. These elements align with 2026 SaaS design trends and make the site feel more modern.
Consolidate and personalise the call‑to‑action (CTA). Simplify the multiple CTAs into a single primary action (e.g., “Get my demo”) and repeat it consistently. Use dynamic or personalised CTAs that reflect user context—for instance, altering the CTA copy based on visitor type or referral source. Keep the CTA button prominent by applying the accent colour.
Strengthen typographic hierarchy and readability. Increase font sizes for subheadings and body text to improve legibility; forum feedback recommends larger type and stronger hierarchy. Avoid mid‑phrase line breaks in the headline and use clear spacing to make content easy to scan. Use no more than two fonts to maintain a cohesive look.
Add social proof and real user context. Include testimonials, pilot customer logos or usage metrics. Social proof helps build trust and differentiates PropFlow from generic SaaS sites. Use examples of actual property managers or agencies using PropFlow, with short quotes about the benefits they’ve experienced.
Restructure the “Who this is for” section and feature list. Present the target audiences—letting agents, estate agents and property managers—as separate cards with a clear statement of their problem and the solution PropFlow provides. Add a dedicated features section that outlines key capabilities (automatic task creation, landlord updates, SLA tracking) with icons or short descriptions to reinforce the benefit.
Optimise for mobile and accessibility. Ensure buttons and interactive elements have sufficient size and spacing for touch devices. Keep page load times low—minimalism helps with speed, but optimise images and use lazy loading. Adjust the colour contrast to meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards for accessibility.
Introduce vibrant accent colours sparingly. While minimalism and neutral tones remain popular, design experts note that the right use of bright colours prevents a site from feeling plain. Use a vibrant accent for highlights or CTAs to draw the eye, ensuring it complements the beige‑green base palette.
Provide interactive product demos and personalised content. Incorporate short video or interactive demos where users can explore PropFlow’s board, perhaps segmented for different use cases (e.g., maintenance requests vs. viewing follow‑ups). This helps visitors see how the platform works and raises confidence in the product.
Simplify and clarify the layout. Keep the minimalist aesthetic but reduce empty space that serves no purpose. Avoid generic stock graphics; instead, use imagery or illustrations that relate directly to property management and workflow automation to give the site a distinct personality.
